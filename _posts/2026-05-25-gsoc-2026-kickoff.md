Hi, I am Aman Kumar. I have been accepted into Google Summer of Code
2026 with HoloViz, under the NumFOCUS umbrella, and I still have to
say that out loud to believe it.

The project is the Lumen and xarray integration, 350 hours of it. My
mentors are Andrew Huang and Andy Maloney.

## The problem, in one sentence

A climate scientist has a NetCDF file: sea surface temperature over
latitude, longitude and time. They want to ask, in plain English,
"what was the average sea surface temperature in the Pacific in
January 2020", and get back a table and a map. With Lumen that should
be a one-liner. Today it is an afternoon of pandas glue code that
flattens the cube and throws the coordinates away on the way in.

Lumen turns a declarative spec into a data app, and
[Lumen AI](https://lumen.holoviz.org) lets you talk to your data. Both
are strongest when the data is a flat table. A lot of real scientific
data is not flat, it is N-dimensional, and the tool for that in Python
is [xarray](https://xarray.dev). Closing that gap is the whole
project.

## What we have done so far

Before GSoC officially starts on May 25, the work is already well
underway. Twenty-six of my pull requests have been merged into Lumen
in the run-up: mostly small bug fixes and quality improvements,
learning the codebase from the inside by paying down its little
papercuts. Fixing a stale-URL bug in download controls, adding a
dedicated Groq LLM provider, tightening the SQL agent against
list-typed `source.tables`, adding `slice` support to `SQLFilter`,
hardening retries, plugging holes in the chat input. That ramp-up
mattered: it is how I went from "outsider with a proposal" to someone
the maintainers were willing to merge from on a 350-hour project.

Two of those merged PRs are the actual foundation of this GSoC
project:

- **[#1741, Add XArraySQLSource for N-dimensional scientific data](https://github.com/holoviz/lumen/pull/1741)**
  (merged 26 Mar 2026). A Lumen Source that wraps xarray Datasets and
  exposes them for SQL querying, with xarray-sql plus DataFusion under
  the hood.
- **[#1791, Wire XArraySQLSource into Lumen AI](https://github.com/holoviz/lumen/pull/1791)**
  (merged 17 Apr 2026). The agent side, so you can actually ask the
  climate-scientist question above through Lumen AI and get something
  back.

So the basic loop from the user story already exists in `main`. The
summer is about making it good, and making it reach.

## What we are planning to do

The honest version: the foundation was the easy twenty percent. The
hard part is everything that makes this usable beyond a demo. The
goals for the 350-hour project, roughly in dependency order:

- **Make it backend-agnostic.** Too much still assumes pandas. Add a
  `DataFrameLike` param type in Param
  ([param#975](https://github.com/holoviz/param/issues/975)) and
  rework Lumen's filters and transforms onto
  [Narwhals](https://narwhals-dev.github.io/narwhals/)
  ([lumen#1788](https://github.com/holoviz/lumen/issues/1788)), so
  Polars, cuDF and others flow through with no conversion. The Polars
  and bioinformatics user stories both die without this.
- **Stop flattening.** Contribute upstream to xarray-sql
  ([#58](https://github.com/xarray-contrib/xarray-sql/issues/58)) so a
  SQL result can round-trip back into a real xarray Dataset with its
  coordinates and attributes intact, instead of a lossy DataFrame.
- **Make data access one command.** A STAC and Pangeo catalog Source,
  so onboarding a climate scientist goes from "download this 4 GB
  NetCDF, then write a YAML" to
  `lumen-ai serve --catalog <stac-url>`.
- **Finish what is already started.** A lead Lumen maintainer has a
  draft for coordinate-aware filtering
  ([lumen#1599](https://github.com/holoviz/lumen/pull/1599)). Bring it
  to completion rather than build a parallel thing next to it, so
  xarray dimensions (time, lat, lon, pressure_level) become first-class
  filterable axes in the Lumen UI.
- **Do not regress the bio path.** Drive a few open issues in
  [lumen-anndata](https://github.com/holoviz-topics/lumen-anndata) to
  closure so the single-cell user story has a real deliverable, and so
  the Narwhals refactor does not quietly break that downstream
  package.
- **Better N-dimensional visualization.** Improve xarray and array
  data support in Vega-Lite
  ([altair#891](https://github.com/vega/altair/issues/891)) and define
  a JSON-serializable HoloViews plot spec
  ([holoviews#791](https://github.com/holoviz/holoviews/issues/791)),
  so the AI agent can produce coordinate-aware charts and ship them
  over the wire.
- **End-to-end tests on real datasets.** Validate the full pipeline
  (load, query, transform, visualize) against a NOAA climate
  reanalysis Zarr in the cloud, a microscopy NetCDF stack, and a
  single-cell AnnData dataset, with Dask-backed lazy computation
  throughout.

I am deliberately not going to land this as one giant pull request at
the end. Each item above is its own reviewable change. Small
reviewable changes beat big impressive ones, and I would rather be
corrected early and often than be clever in private for three months.
